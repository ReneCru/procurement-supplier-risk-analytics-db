# Business Rules

## Project Context

This project models a procurement and supplier risk analytics environment using PostgreSQL.

The database combines public procurement award data with synthetic operational records to simulate a private-sector procurement workflow.

## Core Business Assumptions

### Suppliers

Each supplier represents an organization that received a public procurement award or contract.

Supplier master data may include:

- supplier name;
- country;
- state;
- city;
- supplier type;
- source system;
- source supplier identifier.

### Buyers

Buyers represent the purchasing organization, agency, department, or internal procurement owner.

In the public data source, buyers are mapped from awarding agencies or sub-agencies.

### Categories

Categories represent the type of goods or services purchased.

Categories may be mapped from public procurement classification fields such as NAICS, PSC, or award descriptions.

### Purchase Orders

Public procurement awards are mapped to purchase order headers.

Each purchase order includes:

- supplier;
- buyer;
- category;
- order date;
- required delivery date;
- total amount;
- order status.

### Purchase Order Lines

Because public procurement data does not always provide line-level purchasing detail, purchase order lines are synthetically generated.

Rules:

- Each purchase order may have 1 to 5 lines.
- Line amounts must sum approximately to the purchase order total amount.
- Quantity must be greater than zero.
- Unit price must be greater than or equal to zero.
- Each line belongs to a purchase order.

### Deliveries

Deliveries are synthetically generated from purchase order lines.

Rules:

- Each purchase order line receives one delivery record.
- Scheduled delivery date is based on the purchase order date and expected lead time.
- Actual delivery date may be earlier, on time, or late.
- Delay days are calculated as actual delivery date minus scheduled delivery date.
- Delivery status may be pending, delivered, late, or cancelled.

### Invoices

Invoices are synthetically generated from purchase orders.

Rules:

- Each purchase order may generate one invoice.
- Invoice date must be equal to or later than the purchase order date.
- Due date must be equal to or later than invoice date.
- Payment date must be null or equal to/later than invoice date.
- Invoice status may be open, paid, overdue, or cancelled.

### Compliance Documents

Compliance documents are synthetically generated by supplier.

Document types may include:

- NDA;
- insurance certificate;
- quality certification;
- sanctions screening;
- supplier onboarding form;
- tax document.

Rules:

- Each supplier may have multiple compliance documents.
- Document status may be valid, expired, missing, or pending_review.
- Expiration date must be equal to or later than issue date when both dates exist.
- Expired or missing documents increase supplier risk.

## Supplier Risk Score Logic

The supplier risk score will combine procurement and compliance indicators.

Initial scoring logic:

- Spend exposure: suppliers with higher total spend receive higher exposure scores.
- Late delivery performance: suppliers with more late deliveries receive higher risk scores.
- Compliance status: suppliers with expired or missing documents receive higher risk scores.
- Invoice irregularity: suppliers with overdue invoices receive higher risk scores.

Planned score weighting:

Supplier Risk Score =
40% spend exposure
+ 30% late delivery performance
+ 20% compliance document risk
+ 10% invoice/payment irregularity

## Portfolio Positioning

The goal of the project is to demonstrate:

- relational database design;
- SQL joins;
- data quality constraints;
- analytical indexes;
- procurement KPIs;
- supplier risk analysis;
- business documentation;
- ethical use of public and synthetic data.
