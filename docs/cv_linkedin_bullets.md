## LinkedIn Project Description - English

I built a PostgreSQL procurement and supplier risk analytics database that models suppliers, buyers, categories, purchase orders, purchase order lines, deliveries, invoices, and compliance documents.

The project uses public procurement data from USAspending.gov as the foundation and generates synthetic operational records to simulate private-sector procurement workflows that are not publicly available.

The database includes SQL views and KPI queries for supplier spend, late deliveries, open PO aging, expired compliance documents, monthly purchasing trends, buyer spend, overdue invoices, and supplier risk scoring.

Incluye vistas SQL y consultas KPI para analizar gasto por proveedor, entregas tardías, aging de órdenes abiertas, documentos vencidos, tendencias mensuales, gasto por comprador, facturas vencidas y score de riesgo de proveedores.

## Interview Pitch - English

I created this project to demonstrate how procurement data can be modeled and analyzed in a relational database. I used public procurement award data as the foundation and generated synthetic operational data to simulate private-sector workflows such as purchase order lines, deliveries, invoices, and supplier compliance documents.

The project includes PostgreSQL tables, primary keys, foreign keys, quality constraints, analytical indexes, business views, KPI queries, an ERD, and documented results. It connects SQL and database design with procurement, supplier performance, compliance, and risk analysis.
