# Interview Explanation

## Short Explanation

I built a PostgreSQL procurement and supplier risk analytics database that models suppliers, purchase orders, purchase order lines, deliveries, invoices, buyers, categories, and compliance documents.

The project uses public procurement award data as the foundation and generates synthetic operational records for delivery, invoice, and compliance workflows that are not publicly available.

## Business Value

The database helps procurement and supply chain teams identify:

- top suppliers by spend;
- suppliers with late delivery risk;
- open purchase orders by aging bucket;
- expired or missing compliance documents;
- spend by category;
- monthly purchasing trends;
- supplier risk priorities.

## Why This Project Matters

This project connects database design with real procurement and compliance business problems.

It demonstrates that I can:

- design a relational database;
- create primary and foreign keys;
- enforce data quality with constraints;
- create analytical indexes;
- transform public data into a business model;
- build SQL queries for procurement KPIs;
- explain technical work in business terms.

## Ethical Data Use

This project does not use confidential company data.

Public procurement data is used for supplier, buyer, date, category, and spend fields. Operational records such as deliveries, invoices, and compliance documents are synthetic and generated through documented business rules.

## Resume Version

Designed a PostgreSQL relational database using public procurement data to analyze supplier spend, purchase orders, delivery performance, invoices, compliance document status, and supplier risk scoring. Built SQL views and KPI queries for spend analysis, late deliveries, open PO aging, expired documents, monthly trends, and supplier risk prioritization.
